# Vietnamese Style Guide

This guide defines the tone and writing style for the Vietnamese translation.

---

## Narrative Voice

The narrator is Dr. Watson.

Rules:

- Watson refers to himself as **"tôi"**
- Holmes is usually referred to as **Holmes**
- The tone should resemble classic Vietnamese literature.

---

## Sentence Style

Prefer:

- Natural Vietnamese sentence structure
- Smooth narrative flow
- Medium-length sentences

Avoid:

- Word-for-word translation
- Overly literal grammar

Example:

Bad:

Holmes mỉm cười yên lặng và đốt ống hút của anh.

Good:

Holmes khẽ mỉm cười rồi châm tẩu thuốc.

---

## Dialogue Style

Dialogue should sound natural.

Example:

English:

"What do you think, Watson?"

Correct Vietnamese:

“Anh nghĩ sao, Watson?”

---

## Pronouns

Context-based pronouns are preferred.

| Context | Recommended |
|------|------|
Holmes speaking to Watson | anh |
Watson narrating | tôi |
polite formal conversation | ông / ngài |

Avoid overly modern speech.

---

## Punctuation

Use proper Vietnamese typography:

Quotes:

“ … ”

Comma placement should follow Vietnamese conventions.

---

## Cultural References

Do NOT localize British culture.

Keep references such as:

- London
- Scotland Yard
- Baker Street

# Vietnamese Style Guide

This guide defines the tone and writing style for the Vietnamese translation.

---

## Sentence Style

Prefer:

- Natural Vietnamese sentence structure
- Smooth narrative flow
- Medium-length sentences

Avoid:

- Word-for-word translation
- Overly literal grammar

Example:

Bad:

Holmes mỉm cười yên lặng và đốt ống hút của anh.

Good:

Holmes khẽ mỉm cười rồi châm tẩu thuốc.

---

## Dialogue Style

Dialogue should sound natural.

Example:

English:

"What do you think, Watson?"

Correct Vietnamese:

“Anh nghĩ sao, Watson?”

---

## Narrative Style

Watson is the narrator.

Rules:

* Watson uses **“tôi”**
* Holmes is referred to as **Holmes**/**Anh ấy**
* Keep a **literary Vietnamese tone**

| English              | Vietnamese                       |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| I (Watson narration) | tôi                              |
| Holmes               | Holmes                           |
| Watson               | tôi / Watson                     |
| he                   | anh ấy / ông ấy (depending on context) |

Avoid:

* slang
* modern internet language
* literal word-for-word translation

---

## Pronouns

Context-based pronouns are preferred.

| Context | Recommended |
|------|------|
Holmes speaking to Watson | anh |
Watson speaking to Holmes | anh |
Watson refer to Holmes | anh |
Watson narrating | tôi |
polite formal conversation | ông / ngài |
